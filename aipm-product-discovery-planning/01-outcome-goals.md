# 01 - Outcome Goals

## Primary Outcome Goal
In 3.5 weeks, project managers, PMO leads, and technical founders will be able to turn a project brief into an explainable delay-risk forecast, resulting in at least `60%` of test users saying the forecast increased their confidence in timeline decisions.

## Team Learning Goal
In the same 3.5 weeks, our team will learn to ship a complete `Lovable -> FastAPI -> simulation -> explanation -> analytics` product loop that is measurable, testable, and demo-ready.

## Success Metrics
Primary success metric:
- `>=60%` of test users say the forecast improved decision confidence

Behavior metrics:
- `>=50%` of users run at least one additional simulation or revise the plan after seeing the first result
- `>=40%` of users copy, export, or say they would use the executive summary in a stakeholder update

Product metrics:
- Time to first forecast from project brief is `<2 minutes`
- Forecast response time for sample projects is `<30 seconds`
- End-to-end happy path completes without manual developer intervention in `>=90%` of test sessions

Trust metrics:
- `>=60%` of users rate the explanation as clear enough to defend in a meeting
- Fewer than `20%` of sessions fail because users do not understand inputs or outputs

## Definition of Done
The MVP is done when:
- A user can enter a project brief and receive a validated task plan
- The system returns delay probability, P50, P80, and top risk drivers
- The results page explains why the forecast is risky in plain language
- Feedback and usage events are logged
- At least `5-6` target-user sessions are run and synthesized
- The team can demo the full flow reliably using one real example and one backup sample project

## What Success Looks Like for Users
- Sarah the PM can defend a deadline change with quantified risk instead of gut feel
- Michael the PMO lead can see a standardized risk score and documented assumptions
- Emily the founder can tell whether a roadmap date is realistic before talking to investors

## What Success Looks Like for the Team
- We validate whether the wedge is truly `risk confidence`, not generic planning
- We learn whether explainability is enough to overcome AI trust concerns
- We identify whether next-step demand should focus on integrations, scenario planning, or portfolio reporting
